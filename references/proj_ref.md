# Reference: Nepal Earthquake Project (2015)

## 1. Earthquake Event Overview

The **April 25, 2015 Nepal earthquake**, also known as the **Gorkha earthquake**, was one of the most destructive seismic events in Nepal’s history.

It occurred at **11:56 NST on 25 April 2015** with a magnitude of **Mw 7.8–7.9 (or Ms 8.1)** and a maximum Mercalli intensity of **X (Extreme)**. The epicenter was located near **Barpak in the Gorkha District**, approximately **85 km northwest of Kathmandu**, with a shallow depth of about **8.2 km**.

The earthquake affected **Nepal, India, China, and Bangladesh**, leading to widespread destruction:
- Around **8,962 deaths**
- Approximately **21,952 injuries**
- Large-scale displacement, with hundreds of thousands of people left homeless

### Major Impacts
- Collapse of entire villages across multiple districts  
- Severe damage to UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Kathmandu Valley  
- Destruction of historical structures such as the **Dharahara Tower**  
- Triggering of deadly avalanches:
  - Mount Everest avalanche (22 deaths)  
  - Langtang Valley avalanche (~250 missing)  

Multiple aftershocks followed, including a significant **Mw 7.3 earthquake on 12 May 2015**, which caused additional casualties and damage.

---

## 2. Project Objective

The goal of this project is to analyze building damage data from the 2015 Nepal earthquake and understand the key factors that influenced structural failure.

We aim to build a **classification model** that predicts the **probability of a building suffering severe damage** during an earthquake.

---

## 3. Project Workflow

- Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA) on earthquake building data  
- Identify key features related to structural damage  
- Perform feature engineering and data preprocessing  
- Train a classification model to predict severe damage risk  
- Evaluate model performance using appropriate metrics  

---

## 4. Expected Outcome

The final model will estimate the likelihood of **severe building damage**, helping improve understanding of earthquake vulnerability and supporting disaster risk assessment and preparedness efforts.
